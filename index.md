# Radish — Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** June 26, 2026

Radish is a macro and calorie tracker for iPhone and Apple Watch. It is designed to be
**local‑first**: your information lives on your own device, not on our servers. We do not run a
backend, we do not have user accounts, and we do not collect, sell, or share your personal data.
This policy explains exactly what that means.

## The short version

- **We don't collect your data.** Everything you log stays on your device (and, if you turn it on,
  in your own private iCloud account).
- **No account, no sign‑up, no tracking, no ads, no analytics.**
- The only time Radish talks to the internet is to **look up foods** you scan or search, and to
  **process your one‑time purchase** through Apple. Neither involves sending us your personal
  information.

## Information stored on your device

Radish stores the things you enter so the app can work — for example:

- Foods, meals, and recipes you log, and the date/time and meal they belong to
- Your weight entries and goals
- Your profile inputs for goal estimation (such as height, weight, age, biological sex, and
  activity level) — used only to calculate your targets, on device
- Your settings and preferences

This information is saved locally using Apple's on‑device database. **We have no access to it.**
If you delete the app, the locally stored data is removed with it.

## Optional iCloud sync

If you enable **iCloud Sync** in Settings, Radish uses Apple's **CloudKit** to keep your data in
sync across your devices. That data is stored in **your own private iCloud account**, governed by
Apple's iCloud terms and privacy policy. It is end‑to‑end the user's data — **the developer cannot
see or access it**. You can turn sync off at any time, and you can manage or delete this data
through your iCloud storage settings.

## Apple Health (HealthKit)

If you grant permission, Radish **writes** the nutrition you log (calories, protein, carbohydrates,
fat, and — if you choose to track them — sugar, fiber, sodium, saturated fat, and caffeine) to the
Apple Health app so your nutrition stays in sync there.

- Radish **does not read** your health data.
- Health data is **never transmitted off your device** by Radish; it is handed to Apple Health,
  which you control.
- Data Radish writes to Health is **not** used for advertising or shared with third parties.
- You can revoke Health access at any time in the Health app or iOS Settings, and you can delete
  entries Radish created from within Radish or the Health app.

## Camera (barcode scanning)

Radish uses the camera **only** to scan food barcodes. The camera image is processed on your device
to read the barcode; **images are not stored and not transmitted**.

## Food lookups (Open Food Facts)

When you scan a barcode or search for branded foods, Radish sends **only the barcode number or the
text you searched** to **Open Food Facts** (openfoodfacts.org), a free, open, third‑party food
database, in order to retrieve nutrition facts. These requests **do not include your name, account,
device identifiers, or any of your logged data** — just the barcode or search term. Open Food Facts
has its own privacy policy: https://world.openfoodfacts.org/privacy

Generic foods are searched against a database **bundled inside the app**, which requires no network
connection at all.

## Purchases

Radish is a **one‑time purchase** (no subscription). Payment is handled entirely by **Apple**
through the App Store using your Apple ID. Radish does not receive or store your payment details.
Your purchase restores for free on your devices via your Apple ID. Apple's handling of purchases is
covered by Apple's privacy policy.

## What we do **not** do

- We do **not** collect or transmit your personal information to our own servers (we have none).
- We do **not** use analytics or crash‑tracking SDKs.
- We do **not** show advertising or use advertising identifiers.
- We do **not** track you across apps or websites.
- We do **not** sell or share your data with third parties.

## Children's privacy

Radish is a general‑audience app and is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly
collect personal information from children. Because the app does not collect personal information at
all, no special data is gathered regardless of the user's age.

## Your control and data deletion

- **Local data:** delete entries within the app, or delete the app to remove all locally stored
  data.
- **iCloud data:** turn off iCloud Sync and/or delete the data through your iCloud account.
- **Health data:** manage or delete Radish's entries in the Apple Health app.

## Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, we will update the effective date above and post the revised policy at the
same URL. Material changes will be reflected in an app update where appropriate.
